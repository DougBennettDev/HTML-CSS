The best thing that can be done to improve your guitar playing is effective practice.
What is effective practice?
Effective practice is essentially making the most efficient use of time in an allotted period to maximise results and place you further towards your playing goals
Guitarists are prone to brag (Believe me, I'm definitely guilty of that) and one of the most common things guitarists brag about is how many hours a day they practice
"I play 7 hours a day, and that's why I am literally God's gift to man"
A guitarist who effectively practices for one hour in increments is going to be a better player than the guitarist who aimlessly jams around on their axe hoping to find the next cool riff.
The reason for this is multifold

    -Practicing a concept in a short period of time forces the person to concentrate
    -Concentrated practice increases the brain's imprintation of muscle memory
    -It leaves time in the day to take care of other things in life.

When you incorporate effective practice into a routine, your skills and appreciation of the instrument increases exponentially.
